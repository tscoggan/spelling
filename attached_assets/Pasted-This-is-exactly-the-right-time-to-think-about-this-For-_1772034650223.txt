# ⚖️ First: The Legal Framework (U.S.)

Under **Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)**:

Normally:

* You must get **verifiable parental consent** before collecting personal data from children under 13.

BUT…

There is a **“school consent exception”**:

Schools may provide consent **on behalf of parents** when:

* The service is used for educational purposes
* The school controls student accounts
* Data is used only for educational purposes
* You don’t use the data for behavioral advertising

This is how Google Classroom, Kahoot, etc. operate.

---

# 🏫 Recommended Legal Structure

Yes — your instinct is correct:

> **The school (not the child) should create and control accounts.**

The cleanest structure is:

### 1. School Admin creates the school account

### 2. School Admin invites teachers

### 3. Teachers create student accounts

### 4. Students cannot self-register

This keeps:

* You out of direct minor consent workflows
* The school legally responsible for student consent
* Risk significantly reduced

---

# 🔒 How School Accounts Should Work

## Step 1 — School Admin Registration

The admin:

* Registers using a **school-issued email domain**
* Accepts a “School Agreement” (not standard consumer ToS)
* Certifies they have authority to consent on behalf of the school

You include a required checkbox:

> “I certify that I am authorized to consent on behalf of this school under COPPA.”

That certification matters.

---

## Step 2 — Teacher Accounts

Teachers:

* Must be invited by the admin
* Must use school email
* Cannot convert to personal accounts
* Must agree to Teacher Terms

---

## Step 3 — Student Accounts

Students should:

* Not provide email
* Not provide last names
* Not provide birthdate
* Not have public profiles
* Not have social features

Use:

* First name + last initial OR
* Random display name generated by system
* Internal student ID

The school retains control.

---

# 🧾 Do You Need to Verify That It’s a Real School?

Short answer:

> You do NOT need to perform deep legal verification.
> But you must take reasonable steps.

Regulators expect “reasonable efforts,” not forensic audits.

---

# 🎓 What Counts as “Reasonable Verification”?

### Minimum (Generally Acceptable)

* Require school-domain email (e.g., @lincolnschool.edu)
* Block Gmail/Yahoo/etc.
* Require school name and address
* Include admin certification checkbox

This is what many EdTech startups do initially.

---

### Better (Lower Risk)

* Manual review for new schools
* Verify school appears in public directory
* Confirm website exists
* Send confirmation email to domain
* Require LinkedIn profile of admin

---

### Enterprise-Level (Safest)

* Signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
* Signed School Agreement PDF
* Domain verification via DNS record
* Integration via Google Workspace / Azure AD

---

# 🧠 What You’re Legally Proving

You are not proving the school exists in a court-level way.

You are proving:

* You relied in good faith on school certification
* You required official school emails
* You contractually assigned consent responsibility to the school

That’s what matters.

---

# 🚨 Things You MUST Avoid

Do not:

* Allow kids to convert school accounts to personal accounts
* Allow public sharing of student content
* Use school data for advertising
* Sell student data
* Use tracking ads

Under COPPA school exception:

> Data must be used ONLY for educational purposes.

---

# 📝 Documents You’ll Need

1. School Terms of Service
2. Student Data Privacy Addendum
3. Privacy Policy (separate section for school accounts)
4. Data deletion policy
5. FERPA language (if storing grades/progress)

---

# 🏛️ Don’t Forget FERPA

If schools are involved, you are also indirectly dealing with:

Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)

FERPA governs:

* Educational records
* Student progress
* Who can access student data

You should:

* Allow schools to request data export
* Allow deletion upon request
* Not deny access to student data

---

# 🧩 Recommended Workflow Architecture (Low-Risk Startup Version)

### Public Users (Non-School)

* Parent creates account
* Parent consents for child
* Child profile created under parent

### School Users

* Admin registers
* Domain required
* Admin certification
* Teacher invites
* Student accounts created by teacher only

Two completely separate flows.

This keeps you safe.

---

# 💡 Should You Verify School Admin Identity?

You don’t need ID verification.

You do need:

* School domain email
* Certification checkbox
* Logging of IP and timestamp of agreement

Store that agreement record permanently.

---

# 🔐 Risk Reduction Bonus

Add this clause:

> The School represents and warrants that it has obtained all necessary parental consents under COPPA and applicable law.

That shifts legal exposure.
