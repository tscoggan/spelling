Short answer:

**Legal acceptances must occur *before* Stripe payment is completed.**

You can show pricing first, but you should not process payment until after Terms + Privacy + Parental Consent are affirmatively accepted.

Here’s why and how to structure it properly.

---

# ⚖️ Why Legal Acceptance Must Come First

When a parent pays, they are entering into:

* A contract (Terms of Service)
* A data processing relationship (Privacy Policy)
* A COPPA parental consent certification
* A recurring billing agreement (if subscription)

If you charge first and collect acceptance later:

* The contract formation can be challenged.
* Refund disputes become harder to defend.
* You weaken enforceability of arbitration clauses, refund policy, etc.
* You risk non-compliance with COPPA (consent must precede data collection).

Courts generally enforce **clear clickwrap agreements prior to payment**, not after.

---

# ✅ Correct Workflow for Family Paid Accounts

Here is the legally strongest order:

---

## 1️⃣ Pricing Page (Informational Only)

Show:

* Price
* Billing frequency
* Refund policy summary
* “Continue” button

No charge yet.

---

## 2️⃣ Account Creation

Parent enters:

* Name
* Email
* Password

Email verification recommended.

---

## 3️⃣ Legal Acceptance Screen (Critical Step)

Separate screen with unchecked boxes:

☐ I agree to the Terms of Service
☐ I have read the Privacy Policy
☐ I certify that I am the parent or legal guardian and consent to the collection of my child’s information

Include links to full documents.

Log:

* Terms version
* Privacy version
* Timestamp
* IP address
* User ID

User must click:
**“Continue to Payment”**

---

## 4️⃣ Stripe Checkout

Now redirect to Stripe.

At this stage they have:

* Agreed to contract
* Consented to child data collection
* Accepted billing terms

---

## 5️⃣ Subscription Confirmation

After Stripe success:

* Activate account
* Show receipt
* Send confirmation email

---

# 🧾 Additional Stripe-Specific Requirement

For subscriptions, you must clearly disclose:

* Billing frequency
* Amount
* Auto-renewal
* Cancellation method

This disclosure must appear **before payment submission**.

Best practice: Include a short billing disclosure near the “Continue to Payment” button.

Example:

> You will be charged $X per month. Subscription renews automatically until canceled. Cancel anytime in your account settings.

---

# ❌ What NOT To Do

Do not:

* Hide Terms acceptance inside Stripe metadata
* Rely on Stripe’s terms instead of yours
* Process payment and then ask them to accept
* Pre-check legal boxes
* Combine everything into vague “By continuing…” language

---

# 🔐 Special Note About COPPA Timing

Because your app is directed to children:

Parental consent must occur **before you collect personal information from the child**.

That includes:

* Creating a child profile
* Storing spelling progress
* Saving word lists

So consent must occur:
Before child account creation
Before child data storage
Before payment is finalized (best practice)

---

# 🏗 Clean Implementation Structure (Dev-Ready)

State machine example:

```
Account Status:
- Draft
- Email Verified
- Legal Accepted
- Payment Completed
- Active
```

Only allow:

* Stripe checkout if status = Legal Accepted
* Child profile creation if status = Active

---

# 🎯 Final Recommendation

**Correct order:**

Pricing → Account Creation → Legal Acceptance → Stripe Payment → Account Activation

Not:

Pricing → Payment → Legal Acceptance ❌
