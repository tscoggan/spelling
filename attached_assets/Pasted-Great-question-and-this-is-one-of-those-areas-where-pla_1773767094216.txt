Great question—and this is one of those areas where **placement matters legally**, not just UX.

For a kids app like yours (subject to the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act), your Privacy Policy needs to be **both continuously accessible and explicitly presented at key decision points**.

Here’s the **production-ready placement strategy** I recommend:

---

# 🟢 1. Always Accessible (Required)

These are **non-negotiable**.

## ✅ Website Footer (Every Page)

* Link: **“Privacy Policy”**
* Visible without login
* Same for marketing site + app landing page

👉 This satisfies:

* COPPA “clear and prominent” requirement
* General Data Protection Regulation transparency requirements
* California Consumer Privacy Act notice at collection

---

## ✅ In-App Settings / Menu

* Location: Profile menu or settings screen
* Label: “Privacy Policy”

👉 Required because:

* Users must be able to access it **any time after signup**

---

# 🟡 2. At Account Creation (Critical for You)

This is where many apps mess up.

## ✅ Parent Account Signup (Your “Consent” Step)

You already have this screen (good). It should include:

* Link to Privacy Policy
* Checkbox: “I have read and agree…”
* **Must NOT be pre-checked**

👉 This is your **legal acceptance record**

---

## 🔴 Important Upgrade (Often Missed)

Right now you show:

> “I have read and agree to the Privacy Policy”

That’s good—but for COPPA, you also need:

### 👉 A “Just-in-Time Notice”

Before or on that same screen, add a short summary like:

> “We collect your child’s name, grade level, and learning activity to provide spelling practice. We do not show ads or sell personal data.”

This is specifically expected under COPPA.

---

# 🔵 3. BEFORE Any Child Data Is Collected (Critical)

This is the **#1 commonly missed legal screen**.

Even though the parent consents, you should show:

## ✅ “Add Child” Screen Notice

When parent creates a child account:

Include a short disclosure:

> “By creating a child profile, you consent to the collection and use of your child’s information as described in the Privacy Policy.”

👉 This ties consent directly to **child data creation**, not just account creation.

---

# 🟣 4. School Accounts (Separate Placement)

For school workflows:

## ✅ During School Admin Signup

* Link Privacy Policy
* Link Student Data Privacy Addendum (when you create it)

## ✅ Teacher / Student Context

* Optional but recommended:

  * “This account is managed by your school”

---

# 🟠 5. App Store Requirements (Easy to Miss)

Both stores require this:

## ✅ App Store Listing (External)

* Privacy Policy URL must be provided

For:

* Apple App Store
* Google Play Store

---

# 🧠 Clean Mental Model

Think of it like this:

| Location       | Purpose                  |
| -------------- | ------------------------ |
| Footer         | Always accessible        |
| Settings       | Ongoing access           |
| Signup         | Legal agreement          |
| Child creation | COPPA-specific consent   |
| App store      | Distribution requirement |

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Avoid These)

❌ Only showing policy in footer
❌ Pre-checking consent boxes
❌ No link during signup
❌ No notice when creating child accounts
❌ Burying it behind login

---

# 🟢 Your App (Based on Screens)

You are already **~85% correct**:

### Add just these 2 things:

# 🟢 Final Answer

You should display your Privacy Policy in **5 places**:

1. Website footer (all pages)
2. In-app settings/menu
3. Parent signup (with checkbox)
4. Child account creation (COPPA notice)
5. App store listing
