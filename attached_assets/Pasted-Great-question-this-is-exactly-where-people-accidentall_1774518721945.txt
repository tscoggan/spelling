Great question—this is exactly where people accidentally become non-compliant.

Since you’ll likely use **Wiktionary-based data**, you’re dealing with the **Wikimedia Foundation** license stack:

* Content license: **Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0**
* Sometimes mixed with **GNU Free Documentation License**

---

# ✅ What you are REQUIRED to do

At minimum, your app must:

### 1. Give credit

You must clearly state:

> Definitions sourced from Wiktionary

---

### 2. Link to the source

You should include a link to:

* [https://en.wiktionary.org/](https://en.wiktionary.org/)

(Optional but better: link to the specific word page)

---

### 3. Include the license

You must reference the license and link to it:

* [https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/)

---

### 4. Share-alike (important nuance)

If you **modify definitions**, those modified versions must also be:

* Shared under the same license (CC BY-SA)

👉 If you *don’t modify text*, this is basically a non-issue.

---

# 📱 How to implement this in your app (best practice)

## Option A — Settings / About screen (recommended)

Create a “Legal” or “Attribution” section:

**Example:**

```
Dictionary Data Attribution

Some definitions are sourced from Wiktionary (https://en.wiktionary.org),
licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 License.

https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/
```

---

## Option B — Per-word attribution (strongest compliance)

On each word detail screen:

```
Source: Wiktionary (CC BY-SA 3.0)
```

👉 This is the most legally robust approach, but not strictly required.

---

## Option C — Hybrid (best balance) ⭐

* Per-word: small “Source: Wiktionary”
* Full details: in Legal/Attribution page

👉 This is what most production apps do.

---

# ⚠️ Common mistakes to avoid

### ❌ Hiding attribution in Terms of Service only

Not sufficient—must be **visible to users**

---

### ❌ No clickable links

License requires **reasonable access** to the license text

---

### ❌ Claiming ownership

Never say:

* “Our dictionary”
* “Proprietary definitions”

---

### ❌ Editing definitions without realizing license impact

If you:

* simplify
* rewrite
* combine definitions

👉 That becomes a **derivative work**, which triggers share-alike

---

# 🧠 Practical advice for your app

Since you're building a **spelling app**, I recommend:

### Keep it simple:

* Use definitions as-is (no editing)
* Add attribution once in:

  * Settings → “Data Sources” or “Legal”

### Optional upgrade:

* Add a small “Source: Wiktionary” label on word pages
